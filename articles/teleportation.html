Teleportation and
Quantum Entanglement:

Explained in a nutshell

1 March 2020 - Ayush Garg

A starter guide to understanding quantum entanglement and teleportation! Explore the wierdest parts of quantum realm!

Definition

According to Wikipedia, quantum entanglement “is the physical phenomenon that occurs when a pair or group of particles is generated, interact, or share spatial proximity in a way such that the quantum state of each particle of the pair or group cannot be described independently of the state of the others, even when the particles are separated by a large distance.”

Understanding the concept:

What this means is that quantum entanglement allows particles to linked to each other even if they are millions of light years apart.

This link takes place in the form of co-relation of quantum state of particles. So, lets understand quantum properties first.

For an electron of an atom these include: the principal quantum number (n), the orbital angular momentum quantum number (l), the magnetic quantum number (ml), and the electron spin quantum number (ms) Actually, these describe the position, state and placement of electrons in an atom.

Now For entangled particles, If I put in short, their spin quantum number is exactly opposite to one another. So hypothetically if two of these are placed at distant ends of a galaxy, they will still “communicate” and be linked; In the sense that if spin of one is changed, the latter’s will also change.

The fun part:

Werner Heisenberg proposed that the true state of a particle can never be measured accurately because in the act of measurement we change the state of the particle.

But as we discussed earlier, quantum entanglement makes this possible, somewhat, by accurately measuring the spin of a particle because even if one particle is destroyed in the process, we predict the state of the particle. This is often termed by many as “Spooky action at a distance”(For more detailed explanation read: (Wiki)

This property allows for quantum teleportation. Quantum teleportation is a process in which quantum information (e.g. the exact state of an atom or photon) can be transmitted (exactly, in principle) from one location to another, with the help of classical communication and previously shared quantum entanglement between the sending and receiving location.

Teleportation And The Bright Future:

So we can technically destroy one of the particle to get the exact state of the other entangled particle.

This information than can be used to reconstruct an identical particle thus allowing for teleportation. Our current scientific advancements have a limited coverage on the practical implication of this idea but this idea is solid and the future is bright.
